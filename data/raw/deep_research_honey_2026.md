# Pesticide Residues in South Korean Honey (2020–2026)

Recent Korean studies found neonicotinoid insecticides in domestic honeys. For example, Sardar *et al.* (2025) analyzed 79 honey samples from agricultural, mountainous and urban areas in South Korea and reported widespread contamination with acetamiprid, dinotefuran, flonicamid, imidacloprid, thiacloprid and clothianidin.  Among their results, acetamiprid was detected in 82% of samples (up to 0.152 mg/kg) and flonicamid in 52% (up to 0.19 mg/kg)【25†L81-L87】.  Similarly, Bae *et al.* (2025) measured six neonicotinoids in 30 honeys and found acetamiprid at 176 µg/kg (ppb) in one Korean apple-tree honey (exceeding the EU MRL).  No other neonicotinoids were above quantitation limits.  No official Korean government reports on honey residues were found; all confirmed data come from peer-reviewed analyses.
